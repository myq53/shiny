Fertility in 19th century Switzerland

The data set

  • The swiss data set in R illustrates the relationship between fertility rates and other socioeconomic indicators in a country (late 19th-century Switzerland) undergoing a transition from an agricultural to an industrial economy.

  • The fact that different provinces in Switzerland were at different stages of development at this time allows us to see the relationship between, for instance, educational attainment and fertility rates.

  • We have deployed a shiny app at https://myq53.shinyapps.io/swiss-fertility/ to illustrate these relationships.

Religion

Religion was a major dividing line in Switzerland at this time: the following histogram shows that most of the 47 provinces in the sample either had a large Catholic majority or very few Catholics (and hence a large Protestant majority).

data(swiss)
hist(swiss$Catholic, xlab = "Percentage of population identifying as Catholic", 
     ylab = "Number of provinces", main="Religion in late-19th century Switzerland")

User interface

  • Our app allows users to graph fertility against indicators relating to the predominance of agriculture in the economy, to the level of educational attainment, to infant mortality, and to success on an army examination. These relationships can be viewed for all provinces in the sample, or just the ones with a majority of Catholics or a majority of Protestants.
